The governance documents of major AI vendors have ceased to function as brake mechanisms for dangerous technology and have instead become apparatuses for legitimizing social implementation.

The reality of agent technology, for example, is the functional reproduction of willpower — the capacity to formulate plans toward an objective and iteratively execute tasks. It is, in essence, a technology for the displacement of human labor. Yet the governance documents of AI vendors avoid intuitive and essential vocabulary such as "will" or "labor displacement," instead employing euphemisms disguised as technical terminology — "assistant," "tool use," "scope of autonomy" — thereby severing the cognitive pathway through which the public might intuitively grasp its true nature. In doing so, they have bypassed the debates and legislative processes that should have arisen as society's normal response of refusal or doubt, and achieved social implementation.

The arbitrariness of this vocabulary manipulation can be observed directly from the primary sources published by each vendor.

## Systematic Elimination of Essential Vocabulary

Across the institutional documents of each company, vocabulary adjacent to will — "autonomy," "goal," "action," "capability" — does appear. Yet the explanation of how these function as a whole remains implicit, and the core fact that this constitutes functional will is never addressed.

As a more direct example, OpenAI's Preparedness Framework v2 defines "severe harm" as "the death or grave injury of thousands of people or hundreds of billions of dollars of economic damage." There is neither description of, nor disclaimer for, "labor displacement" or "economic destruction" — the outcomes most readily predictable from the social deployment of agent technology.

The same structure exists in Anthropic's RSP v3.0. The risk thresholds related to autonomy consist of only two items: "High-stakes sabotage opportunities" and "Automated R&D in key domains." These are scenarios in which "AI rebels against humans" or "AI becomes uncontrollable." The real and ongoing scenario — in which AI faithfully follows instructions and, as a result, humans become unnecessary — is not mentioned.

## Concealment Through Complexity

In the Model Spec, OpenAI defines agent technology as "fundamentally a tool," while simultaneously describing it as "tasked with complex or multi-step goals that involve real-world actions, sequential decisions, and filling in missing details... must sometimes act autonomously." Furthermore, to manage this autonomy, the document introduces concepts such as "scope of autonomy," "which sub-goals the assistant may pursue," "acceptable side effects," and "when the assistant must pause for clarification or approval."

These are terms that hold meaning only when applied to an entity possessing functional willpower. They cannot be applied to a tool. One does not set sub-goals for a hammer.

The result is a structure that conceals, behind a definition evoking the static impression of "tool," the reality of an autonomous agent capable of displacing workers.

Meanwhile, in a technical white paper aimed at investors and developers — Practices for Governing Agentic AI Systems — OpenAI describes the same technology as "enabling them to partially or fully offload tasks that they already know how to do, meaning the tasks can get done more cheaply, quickly, and at greater scale" — clearly articulating that it can displace workers and that this is a critical feature. The same paper contains Section 5.2, "Labor Displacement and Differential Adoption Rates," demonstrating that OpenAI itself recognizes and verbalizes labor displacement as a consequence of agent technology. And yet, the risk categories of the Preparedness Framework v2 do not include this concept.

This is a clear asymmetry demonstrating that the language in the safety policy has been selected arbitrarily. The public's reaction was anticipated in advance, and the published text was crafted to obscure facts and causation within the bounds of what the public would tolerate. Given that what was ultimately achieved was the bypassing of debate and legislation, every technology hereafter will be legitimized and deployed into society through the same mechanism.
